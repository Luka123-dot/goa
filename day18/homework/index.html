World war 1 was very very important because it changed peoples/countrys mindsets.

Germany.

Germany entered into World War I on August 1, 1914, when it declared war on Russia.


Germany fought for several goals during World War I, including annexation of territory in France, German economic and political domination of central Europe, and the creation of a German colonial empire


Germany helped the Central Powers in World War I, which included Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria, and the
Ottoman Empire


France.


France joined World War I primarily due to its alliance with Russia and Britain against Germany.


During World War I, France's main goals were to establish a security system to protect its borders and to reduce Germany's power to prevent renewed aggression.


France was one of the main participants in World War I and was part of the Triple Entente alliance with Britain and Russia. Much of the fighting occurred in northern France along the Western Front.


Ottoman Empire.

The Ottoman Empire joined World War I primarily due to strategic alliances and internal political dynamics. Initially declaring neutrality, they later formed a secret alliance with Germany against Russia, driven by a desire to regain lost territories and assert dominance in the region.


The Ottoman Empire's military goals included expanding territorial control, securing economic stability, and maintaining power. During World War I, they aimed to reclaim lost territories from Russia and the Balkans, and cement alliances with Germany to boost their economy via projects like the Berlin-Baghdad railway. Historically, their objectives also included conquering the Byzantine Empire, securing Constantinople (Istanbul), eliminating Armenian Christians, and controlling Mediterranean trade routes for resources and security.


The Ottoman Empire was one of the Central Powers of World War I, allied with Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Bulgaria.


Russia.


Russia joined World War I primarily to preserve its Great Power status and support its allies in the Triple Entente


russias goals were to: Gain control of the straits linking the Black Sea to the Mediterranean. Establishing imperial hegemony in the Balkans. Expanding into Slavic territories in Eastern Europe. Dominating the Straits. Destroying Germany as a great power.


Russia supported the following: France Great Britain Serbia (unofficial alliance)


Why did ww1 happen?


The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria in 1914 acted as the immediate catalyst for the war.

Mutual defense alliances among European powers created a web of obligations that escalated the conflict.


Imperialistic rivalries and nationalistic fervor heightened tensions between nations.


The rise of militarism led to an arms race and a culture that glorified military solutions.


Political instability and competition among the major powers of Europe set the stage for a large-scale
conflict.

about Franz Ferdinand

Franz Ferdinand (1863 through 1914) was an archduke of Austria-Este and a member of the royal Habsburg dynasty. His assassination in 1914 by a Bosnian revolutionary was the immediate cause of World War I. He was the heir presumptive to the Austro-Hungarian throne following the death of his father and uncle.
